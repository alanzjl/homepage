BAXNECT &
BAXTER THE BOXER

We enable baxter to mimic human behaviors
in dual-arm or single arm modes together with recording functionality

Overview

We use Kinect to record the motion of human arms. After transformation and mapping, we send commands to Baxter. Baxter will carry out the same action as Kinect recording.

By using our Baxnect system, hopefully we will no longer need the teaching pedant to set the actions and rules for industrial robotic arms. Robots can ‘learn’ from people simply in a more natural way.

MAIN FUNCTIONS

1. Use two arms to control two Baxter arms separately.  A user moves his/her two arms to control the actions of Baxter’s two arms. Since Kinect can only get 4 DOF data from human arms, users can only control 4 DOF (7 DOF in total )of Baxter arms.

2. Two human arms control one arm (with a gripper) of Baxter. Since Baxter arms have 7 DOF while Kinect can only provide 4 DOF for each arm, we could not use one arm to control one Baxter arm with a gripper. Thus, we can use 7 DOF out of 8 DOF of two human arms to control a Baxter arm and then use one DOF of a shoulder to control the gripper.

3. Face Recognition. The system can recognize the user and display it on the screen of Baxter.
