TenniMate - Overall

TenniMate is a tennis assistant system that can automatically collect and serve tennis balls. This system consists of two independent robots: TenniMate Collector and TenniMate Server. TenniMate Server is the serving robot in the system. It serves tennis balls with polyurethane wheel groups at a muzzle velocity 196km/h. Collector is the collecting robot with a rotary drum attached with nylon yarn. The Collector uses a camera installed in the front of the body to detect the position of tennis balls automatically. With Computer Vision (CV) technology, after splitting colors, finding contours, Hough transform algorithms, TenniMate can detect the position of tennis balls accurately. Then the motion control module begins to calculate the moving strategy with PID self-adjusting algorithm. TenniMate uses Intel Minnow Board Turbot as the main controller embedded with a lightweight operating system Arch Linux, which operates fast. In order to sync the real-time communication between nodes, ROS (Robot Operating System) is used. Because of the high complexity of CV algorithms, this system adopts hardware acceleration with Intel HD Graphics to improve the efficiency. Users can control TenniMate in two ways: voice controlling and network controlling. With IFLY Voice Recognition Platform, users can directly say “Collect”, “Serve”, or “Start/Stop” to control the system. Structure Design

TenniMate series consists of two robots: TenniMate collector, the collecting robot, and TenniMate Server, the serving robot.



The shell of the robot is made by organic plexiglass, which is light weight and also beautiful. At the front of the shell there is a camera mounting part, on which the camera and electronic devices can be installed. The shell and the skeleton can grip each other tightly and the shell is easy to install and uninstall easily. The shell is strong enough not to be crushed by high speed tennis balls. The chassis consists of two glass fiber boards and copper double-screw bolts that hold the boards together. Electronic devices such as control boards, circuits, cables, batteries, as well as the motors can be installed between the boards. Frosted plexiglass surrounds the chassis so that the chassis can stay beautiful and strong.



The supporting parts of the rotary drum is designed to be made by carbon fiber as well as glass fiber. Carbon fiber is used on places that have more loading and more holes. Every supporting pillar consists of four fiber boards. There are teeth on the edge of each board so they can be attached to each other more tightly.



The ball storage tank consists of four frosted grey plexiglass on which our logo is printed. The tank is connected to the chassis by two clips which makes the tank easy to install and uninstall, and remain beautiful at the same time.


Operation Principle

TenniMate Collector is driven by two wheel in the front of it. And there is an Omni-directional wheel which has no driven force at the back of the chassis. When the robot moves forward, the rotation speed and direction of the two front wheels are the same. When the robot make turns, the front wheels rotates in the same direction but at different speeds, so that the robot can make smooth turns with any radius.

The rotary drum is connected to the motor on the chassis by a belt. Soft brush is attached to the drum, so that when the drum rotates, tennis balls can be swept into the storage tank. The skeleton of the Server is made of aluminum alloy, which is strong and extreme durable. The weight of the skeleton make the server difficult to move for the reason of the recoil force during serving, and ensured the precision of serving.

The serving mechanism consists of four pu plastic wheels. Each two of them are placed side by side in a group. Each group of the wheels are connected to the skeleton by a stainless steel chaft and two bearings. Because the distance between the two group of the wheels are a little bit smaller than the radius of a tennis ball, the tennis ball can receive a friction force as big as possible, so that the ball can gain a high speed in a very short time and the range as well as the speed of the serve is increased. Each group of the wheels are driven by a 12volt DC brush motor at a speed of 10000rpm. The shaft and the rotor are connected by belts. The motors are driven by a L298N chip and controlled by an Arduino board.



Rolling bearings are used at the connecting places of the shaft and the wheels as well as the chassis. In this way, the friction can be reduced greatly and less energy is wasted during working. The ball storage tank is in the shape of a rectangular pyramid. At the size of 400*300*300mm, the tank has the capacity of more than 100 tennis balls. The tank is attached to the skeleton with four screws, so that the tank won’t flip over even when it’s full loaded. Under the tank, there is a specially designed rotary ball deliver. The shape of the deliver is like a revolving door. The tennis ball falls into the hole on the deliver from the tank, then the deliver rotates 180 degree so the tennis ball inside align with the hole on the bottom board. The tennis ball then fall down into the pipe beneath. With this deliver, only one tennis ball can go into the pipe at a time. So that the server will not be jammed by tennis balls. And by controlling the rotating speed of the deliver, the frequency of serving can also be controlled. The deliver is driven by a worm gear rotor and a L298N controller.

Controlling Strategy

For the motion control part, it consists of three subsystems, as you can see in the diagram:

The priority is Web Control > Voice Control > Vision Control. Vision control is a key step to accomplish self-navigation and further operations. For this subsystem, the goal is clear, which is to identify the tennis ball. To accomplish this, we decide to use the OpenCV library (short for Open Source Computer Vision Library). It has tons of API to help us with the recognition. The whole recognition process consists of three states. The first state is pre-processing. The system gets the image information from our camera at first, then it converts the RGB channels to HSV (Hue, Saturation, and Value) channels. By omitting V channel data, we can eliminate the disturbance caused by the difference of lighting. This step is important for the accuracy of recognition. Then the image is converted back to RGB channels to generate the histogram of the image. By making a graying process, the system can eliminate other colors by comparing the color of the object with the color of a tennis ball which is already preset. After eliminating the irrelevant color, we come to the second state, scanning. In this state, the system scans the whole grey graph. We applied a new method called Hough Transform to the process to get the circle shape. So after getting the color and shape, we can find our ball roughly. Then comes to the final state, tracking. When the system is working, there might be situations that tennis balls are collided and moving. So in advance we added this process to track a moving ball. We use an algorithm called Camshift (short for Continuously Adaptive Mean-Shift) to calculate each frame in the video stream to get the moving ball.

To make the remote control to the robot easier, we used voice control technology. The voice control system is accomplished by using Fly Mobile Speech Platform, which is one of the most advanced engine to deal with Chinese language. During the whole process, we use two method to accomplish the communication part, IAT and TTS. IAT is short for iFly Auto Transform, which is used to accept the user’s voice input, return an interactive result automatically based on the input. Because we get the response in word and we need to broadcast it in voice, so we use another interface named TTS (short for Text To Speech). This interface accept the characters as input, and it will transform it in audio data. By playing the audio generated by the platform, the whole process is accomplished. With this subsystem, we can accomplish many things using voice control. For instance, you can instruct the robot to pick up a tennis ball by saying ‘Pick up balls for me!’ And you will hear a response like ‘Yes sir!’, and the robot will do its job.

One last control system is based on web. If the user want to control the robot manually, he can logon the local website via the wireless network generated by a router on the robot. On the website the user can get information about the design team, the product and also the real-time video streaming of the camera. You can control the robots to go wherever you want, to pick or serve balls whenever you want with the help of the video streaming. To set up the web server, we chose flask as the backend, which is a web framework based on python. To get the real time video streaming, we used another tool named socket.io, which is an engine to set up a bridge for backend and frontend, and easy to use. The real time image data can be passed from backend to frontend by the engine so that a real time video can be displayed on the website. To control the robot, we designed several buttons on the user interface. By simply tapping on the direction button, the user can control the robot to move in any direction. The POST method is captured at backend to get which button the user pressed, so the command can be received.

With the help of the whole network system, controlling the robot manually is as easy as several taps on your smart phone screen. The website is on local network so that the user doesn’t need access to the Internet.

Basic registers and their addresses

Register Instructions

Code Function
0000 xx xx LOAD TMP, {DX, xxxx}
0001 RD xx STORE RD, {DX, xx}
0010 RD xx MOV RD, TMP

The 12-bit address is made up of 4 bits from the current instruction and 8 bits from the next instruction.

Branch Instructions

Code Function
1100 OFFSET Branch Zero
1101 OFFSET Branch Carry
1110 OFFSET Branch Overflow
1111 OFFSET Branch Negative
0011 1111 HALT

For Branch instructions, address of the next instruction is the sum of OFFSET and the address of the current instruction. OFFSET is a signed number between -8 and 7

Arithmetic Logic Unit (ALU) Instructions

Addition:

Code Function
1000 RD xx RD -> RD + 1
1001 RD xx RD -> RD - 1
1010 RD RS RD -> RD + RS
1011 RD RS RD -> RD - RS

Multiplication:

Code Function
0100 RD RS (unsigned) RD -> RD[3:0] * RS[3:0]
0101 RD RS (signed) RD -> RD[3:0] * RS[3:0]

Division:

Code Function
0110 RD RS (unsigned) RD[3:0] -> RD[7:0]/RS[3:0] MOD: RD[7:4]
0111 RD RS (signed) RD[3:0] -> RD[7:0]/RS[3:0] MOD: RD[7:4]

Flags

Position Function
0000000x ZF: zero flag
000000x0 CF: carry flag
00000x00 OF: overflow flag
0000x000 NF: negative flag

PFCal - A Power Flow Calculator

A power flow calculator based on Newton-Raphson method. Work with PQ, PV and Vt buses.


Introduction

PFCal is a power flow calculator that supports PQ bus (with P and Q known), PV bus (with P and amplitude U known) and Vt bus (with amplitude and phase angle of generator known). The main goal is to simplify the calculation with guaranteed accuracy.

Based on Visual Studio MFC, and Eigen.

Attention

Calculation with Vt buses and PQ buses has been fully tested, but because of our lack of samples, calculation with PV buses has only been tested few times. Also, we found out that sometimes projects with Pv buses will go trapped.
The Accuracy: within 1e-5.

Please pay attention to the unit of your input.
