Theory

Forces and Kinamatics

Assumptions
Quasi-static assumption: We assume that the agent and the object moves slowly, so that the dynamic effects such as acceleration can be negligible. The force and torque due to the contact motion balance out the friction at all times.
Uniform friction and density assumption: We assume that the friction between the object and the supporting surface, and the density of the object are uniform. The center of mass of the object corresponds to its geometric center and is directly above the friction center.​
Perfect actuator assumption: We assume the motion of the agent is accurate, say, there is no error accumulated.

System Dynamics
Before working on the object and agent, This paper analyzes the force condition of the system. Two kinds of friction forces and one contact force are demonstrated to exist in this system. Here we mainly explore the two friction forces:
​Contact friction between object and agent, via point contact: Contact friction force is parallel to the contact surface. Magnitude of this friction force determines whether the agent slides along the object surface. If the magnitude of this friction force is larger than the product of the friction coefficient of contact surface and contact force, a sliding between agent and object will exist, and vice versa. 
Friction between object and supporting surface:  When the object translates, the sliding friction opposes the motion, the direction of which is the connection line between the contact point and the center of mass of the object; when the object rotates, friction causes a frictional rotation torque around the center of friction.
The Contact Friction Cone
The contact friction only exits within the contact friction cone, which is determined by the friction coefficient of the contact point. We assume the agent can exert any amount of force at the contact. If the push force lies within the friction, the contact force equals the push force and there will be no slip between the object and agent. If the push force is outside of the friction cone, the agent will slide and the push force can be divided into a force along the center line of the friction cone and a force along the surface which generates the slip.
Object Kinematics
 Resulted motion of a push each push force can be divided into a force along the connecting line of contact point and the center of mass, and a force vertical to it. The parallel force results in translation and the vertical force results in rotation.  We only plan pushes that lie within the friction cone, in which case there will be no slip between the agent and the object.
​ Motion of the object The instantaneous motion of the object due to a point-contact push can be divided into a translation along CG, the connecting line between the contact point and the mass center, together with a rotation around G, the center of mass. When there is no slip between agent and object and we ignore acceleration, the pushing distance CC’ can be acquired accurately by controlling the robot.
